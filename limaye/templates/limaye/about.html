Anil Limaye graduated with B.Pharm (1997) and M.Tech (1999) degrees from Jadavpur University. He then moved to Bangalore for his doctoral research under the supervision of Prof. Kondaiah at IISc, Bangalore. He earned his Ph.D in the year 2006 with his thesis titled "Androgen regulated gene expression in the rat ventral prostate: differential regulation by orchidectormy and flutamide treatment". Following short stints at TIFR and JNCASR between 2006 and 2008, he joined the Department of Biosciences and Bioengineering (formerly known as the Department of Biotechnology) at IIT Guwahati as an Assistant Professor. His is a Molecular Endocrinology lab and his group's research is aimed at understanding hormone regulated gene expression, hormone signaling and mechanisms that underlie development and progression of endocrine cancers.

Teaching is the other very important part of his job at IIT Guwahati. He takes courses for B.Tech, M.Tech and Ph.D students. The courses he has taken till date include, BT300 (a seminar course for B.Tech students), BT201 (Biochemistry), BT208 (Molecular Biology and Genetic Engineering), BT502 (Quantitative Biology), BT604 (Enzymology) and BT633 (Human Biology and Disease).
